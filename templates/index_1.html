Research Focus

My current research delves into the complexities of convection and turbulence within complex terrains and transitional regimes. This work aims to unravel the stochastic nature of these atmospheric phenomena, improving our understanding and prediction of weather patterns in challenging environments.

Previous Projects

In a notable past project, we developed a machine learning emulator to estimate downwelling longwave radiation over the Himalayas. This initiative was crucial for regions where direct measurements of longwave radiation were sparse or unavailable. By leveraging only basic meteorological parameters, our tool enabled accurate calculations of this essential radiation component, providing valuable insights into atmospheric conditions and energy balance.
